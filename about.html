About

I work with both analogue and digital photography, drawn to the textures of light, time, and place. My practice is shaped by a background that moves between observation and documentation—whether through a camera, on an excavation, or in the details of historical research.

Background

Alongside photography, I work as a surveyor and also hand-paint lettering on borderstones—a meticulous craft that connects measurement and mark-making. I studied art history and archaeology, with experience on excavations, and have done historical research for an artist's work, looking into the layers of the past before they were transformed into art.

These different ways of seeing—surveyor, photographer, researcher—inform one another. The land, its boundaries, its histories, and its images all come into focus.
