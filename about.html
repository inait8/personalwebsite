About

I work with both analogue and digital photography. My practice is shaped by a background that moves between observation and documentation—whether through a camera, on an excavation, or in the details of historical research.

Background

Alongside photography, I work as a surveyor and do hand-paint lettering on borderstones. I work extensively with GIS programs (Geographic Information Systems), mapping landscapes, archaeological sites, and boundaries. The precision of digital mapping complements the observational slowness of photography—both ways of understanding place.

I studied art history and archaeology, with experience on excavations, and have done historical research for an artist's work, looking into the layers of the past before they were transformed into art.

These different ways of seeing—surveyor, photographer, researcher, GIS analyst—inform one another. The land, its boundaries, its histories, and its images all come into focus.
